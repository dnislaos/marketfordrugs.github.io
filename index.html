OUR GOAL

Discouraging consumption can save lives down the supply chain.

The health consequences of consuming drugs are widespread but the social and economic losses are much greater and difficult to estimate. Attention in the media is constantly given to cases of arrest or prosecution of drug traffickers, but little is portrayed regarding the impact of the drug trafficking network on global welfare. Regardless on your stand on legalization, we encourage you to think about how illegal drug trafficking is still taking lives around the world.

"While the mayority of drugs are produced in developing countries, consumption is centered on high-income nations. Only 12 hectares of land are used to produce drugs (cannabis) in wealthy countries. This represent less than 0.01% of the total area of cannabis cultivation worldwide. At the same time, 12 out of the 15 countries with highest consumption of cannabis in the globe are high-income nations and 14 out of the 15 countries with the biggest coca consumption are wealthy countries."
